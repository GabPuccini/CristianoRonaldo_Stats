Career timeline

Cristiano Ronaldo through the years

From a teenager in Lisbon to the road to 1,000 goals: the whole story, milestone by milestone. Scroll through 24 years of history.

Updated July 2026
Back to stats

  1. Sporting CP 2002 to 2003

    1. August 2002

      The boy from Madeira

      At 17 he broke into the Sporting CP first team, debuting against Inter in a Champions League qualifier before his first senior goals arrived that autumn. Within a year, the biggest club in England came calling.

  2. Manchester United 2003 to 2009

    1. 16 August 2003

      Old Trafford calls

      United paid £12.24m for the teenager and Sir Alex Ferguson handed him the number 7 shirt. Four days after signing, he tore Bolton apart as a substitute on his debut.

    2. 22 May 2004

      The first trophy

      A 44th minute header opened the scoring against Millwall in Cardiff, and the FA Cup became the first medal of a career that would gather them by the armful.

    3. 12 June 2004

      First for Portugal

      At 19 he scored his first international goal, against Greece in the opening game of Euro 2004, as host nation Portugal marched all the way to the final.

    4. 21 May 2008

      King of Europe in Moscow

      His header put United ahead in the Luzhniki final against Chelsea, won on penalties for his first Champions League, crowning a 42 goal season in the middle of three straight Premier League titles.

    5. 2 December 2008

      Ballon d'Or number one

      Named on all 96 ballots, he won his first Ballon d'Or at 23. It would not be the last.

  3. Real Madrid 2009 to 2018

    1. 6 July 2009

      The €94m Galactico

      Real Madrid paid a then world record €94m (£80m) and presented him to a packed Bernabeu. Nine years of impossible numbers were about to begin.

    2. 2 May 2012

      The league of records

      Madrid took the 2011/12 La Liga title with 100 points and 121 goals, 46 of them his, the most prolific league season of his career.

    3. 24 May 2014

      La Decima

      In the 121st minute in Lisbon he scored Madrid's fourth against Atletico to seal the club's tenth European Cup. His 17 goals that season are still the record for a single Champions League campaign.

    4. 10 July 2016

      Champion of Europe with Portugal

      A Payet challenge ended his final after 25 minutes, so he spent extra time coaching from the touchline instead, and Eder's winner gave Portugal their first major title. The tears at kick off became tears of joy.

    5. 7 December 2017

      Five Ballon d'Ors

      The awards for 2013, 2014, 2016 and 2017 joined the first from 2008, a collection to match anything the game had ever seen.

    6. 3 April 2018

      The bicycle kick in Turin

      His overhead kick for Madrid against Juventus was so outrageous that the home fans stood to applaud an opponent. Months later, they signed him.

    7. 26 May 2018

      Three in a row

      Milan 2016 on his winning penalty, a Cardiff brace in 2017, then Kyiv 2018: five European Cups in all, with a Club World Cup final hat trick along the way.

  4. Juventus 2018 to 2021

    1. 10 July 2018

      The €117m Juventus move

      Juventus paid a €100m fee rising to €117m with add ons, making him, at 33, the most expensive signing in Serie A history. Two league titles and a scoring crown followed.

    2. Summer 2021

      King of the Euros

      The Golden Boot at Euro 2020 with five goals took him to 14 at European Championships, the all time record for the tournament.

  5. Return to Manchester 2021 to 2022

    1. 1 September 2021

      The greatest international scorer

      Two late goals against Ireland in Faro, his 110th and 111th, carried him past Ali Daei as the top scorer in the history of men's international football.

    2. 11 September 2021

      Home again

      Signed on deadline day and back in the 7, he scored twice against Newcastle on his second Old Trafford debut, twelve years after leaving.

  6. Al Nassr 2023 to now

    1. January 2023

      A new frontier

      Announced on 30 December 2022, he made his debut as Al Nassr captain in January and put Saudi football on the world's screens overnight.

    2. 5 September 2024

      Goal 900

      A Nations League strike against Croatia in Lisbon made him the first man in history to reach 900 career goals.

    3. 8 June 2025

      Nations League, again

      His equaliser against Spain in the Munich final forced penalties, and Portugal lifted the trophy for a second time.

    4. 21 May 2026

      Champion in a fourth country

      A final day brace against Damac sealed Al Nassr's title and his first Saudi crown, adding Saudi Arabia to league titles in England, Spain and Italy.

    5. 23 June 2026

      Six World Cups

      A brace against Uzbekistan made him the first man to score at six World Cups and Portugal's all time World Cup scorer, in the tournament he confirmed would be his last.

    6. July 2026

      The road to 1,000

      He stands at 976 career goals. The last great number is 24 away, and the dashboard is tracking every one.
